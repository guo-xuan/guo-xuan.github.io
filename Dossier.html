Contributions to Science

  • Metaproteomics Search
    • Metagenomics and metaproteomics can characterize complex microbial communities. However, metagenome assemblies often generate enormously and yet incomplete protein databases, which undermines the identification of peptides and proteins in metaproteomics. Dr. Guo's group has developed a series of tools for identifying proteins from microbes. (1) Sipros Ensemble was designed for database searching using an ensemble approach. Benchmarking with soil and marine microbial communities demonstrated a higher number of peptide and protein identifications by Sipros Ensemble than popular proteomics search tools. Sipros Ensemble was computationally efficient and scalable on supercomputers. Sipros Ensemble was applied to microbial communities in P-deficient soils and P-rich soils, from which microbial communities were found to enhance the degradation of P-containing nucleic acids and phospholipids and the decomposition of labile carbon and nitrogen substrates. In contrast, microbial communities in the P-rich soils promoted the degradation of recalcitrant aromatic compounds, the transformation of nitrogenous compounds, and the assimilation of sulfur from taurine. (2) MetaLP was developed by Dr. Guo's group for inferring microbial proteins given a list of peptides using an integrative linear programming model. The protein inference task was formulated as an optimization problem that can be effectively solved using linear programming. Taxonomic abundance information, which was extracted from whole-genome sequencing or 16s rRNA gene sequencing, was incorporated as prior information in MetaLP. Benchmarking with mock, human gut, soil, and marine microbial communities demonstrated a higher number of protein identifications by MetaLP than the ProteinLP, PeptideProphet, DeepPep, and Sipros Ensemble frameworks. MetaLP substantially improved protein inference in complex metaproteome samples.

  • Detecting Gene-Gene Interactions in Genome-wide Case-Control Studies
    • Large genome-wide association studies (GWASs) have been considered to hold promise for unraveling complex relationships between genotype and phenotype. Dr. Guo’s group has developed many fast and powerful methods, such as JS-MA, TS, DCHE, DAM, MSCD, etc., for detecting genome-wide interactions related to multiple genes under different scenarios. Experimental results on simulated data demonstrate that these methods were more robust than some recently proposed methods. These methods have confirmed and discovered many novel high-order associations significantly rich in cases with different diseases, including Age-related Macular Degeneration, Rheumatoid Arthritis, and Type 1 Diabetes.

  • Metagenomics Sequence Assembling
    • In metagenomics, the population sequencing is the approach to recover the genomic sequences in a genetically diverse environment. Dr. Guo has developed a novel metagenomic sequence assembly framework for achieving a trade-off between the noise of the assembled contigs and the gain in the sequence length for better annotation for large-scale sequencing projects. The systematical comparison with other popular short read assembly programs on both synthetic and real metagenomic sequence datasets demonstrate that the novel framework not only partitions the sequence reads with extremely high accuracy, but also reconstructs more bases, generates higher quality assembled contigs, and yields higher assembly scores with a nearly linear speed-up.
    1. Guo, X., Yu, N., Ding, X., Wang, J. and Pan, Y., 2015. Dime: a novel framework for de novo metagenomic sequence assembly. Journal of Computational Biology, 22(2), pp.159-177. https://doi.org/10.1089/cmb.2014.0251
